### 112 — The Complete Sleep Supplement Guide: Beyond Melatonin  *(Guide)*
- **Status:** Material correction to magnesium-sleep claim.
- **Fact-check error caught:** Original text claimed "A 2022 meta-analysis of 19 RCTs found magnesium supplementation significantly improved subjective sleep quality, sleep efficiency, and sleep onset latency, particularly in individuals with baseline deficiency." Per the Arab et al. 2022/2023 systematic review (PMID 35184264 [funder: none_disclosed], DOI 10.1007/s12011-022-03162-1), the review actually included **9 studies** (mix of cross-sectional, cohort, and RCTs) totalling 7,582 subjects, and **the RCTs reported contradictory findings** with the authors concluding the association is "uncertain" and calling for larger, longer trials. Rewrote that paragraph to honestly reflect the review's actual scope and conclusion.
- **Other claims:** Glycine 3 g (Bannai 2012), L-theanine 100–200 mg, PS 400–600 mg, low-dose melatonin 0.3–1 mg — all retained, all consistent with the literature.
- **Sources:** Expanded from 3 → 5, all with PMIDs.

### 113 — Folate vs Folic Acid: Why the Form Matters  *(Breakthrough)*

Papakostas 2012 L-methylfolate trial confirmed (PMID 23212058 [funder: none_disclosed]). Prinz-Langenohl 2009 confirmed (PMID 19761456 [funder: none_disclosed]).
- **Sources:** Expanded from 3 → 4, all with PMIDs.

### 115 — Kratom: The Opioid Supplement Hiding in Plain Sight  *(Safety — extra rigor applied)*
- **Status:** Safety rewrite with expanded sourcing per safety-category requirement.
- **Fact-check:**
  - 7-HMG mu-opioid binding higher than morphine: confirmed in Hemby et al. 2018 (PMID 29949228 [funder: public], DOI 10.1111/adb.12639) and Kruegel & Grundmann 2018 (PMID 29074406).
  - Updated language from "potency" to "affinity" + animal self-administration data — more defensible.
  - DEA 2016 Schedule I proposal withdrawal: confirmed (Federal Register 2016).

  - CDC death data: Olsen et al. 2019 MMWR (PMID 30973850 [funder: none_disclosed · COI]) — kratom detected in 152 of 27,338 unintentional drug-overdose deaths in 27 states July 2016–Dec 2017.
- **Sources:** Expanded from 3 → 8 to meet safety-category minimum. All sources are peer-reviewed primary literature, regulatory documents, or established surveillance datasets.

### 116 — Colostrum: Ancient Superfood or Influencer Hype?  *(Reality Check)*
- **Status:** Updated primary citation; clarity rewrite.
- **Fact-check:** Original cited Davison 2012 as "meta-analysis"; that publication is a chapter, not a meta-analysis. The actual systematic review and meta-analysis is Jones et al. 2016 (PMID 27462401 [funder: none_disclosed], DOI 10.1186/s13102-016-0047-8): 5 RCTs, 152 participants, 44% reduction in URS days and 38% in URS episodes over 8–12 weeks of training. Updated text to reflect the correct primary source and effect sizes.
- **Marketing claim caveats** preserved (no skin/dewy-glow trials, healthy-adult evidence is thin, IGF-1 is gut-degraded).
- **Sources:** Expanded from 3 → 4, replaced Davison chapter with Davison 2021 *Nutrients* review (PMID 33918655 [funder: none_disclosed · COI]).

- **Sources:** Expanded from 3 → 4. Replaced 1982 Hallberg with 1989 Hallberg/Brune/Rossander (better review article on the iron-vitamin C interaction).

### 120 — Green Tea Extract and Liver Failure: What You Need to Know  *(Safety — extra rigor applied)*
- **Status:** Safety rewrite with expanded sourcing per safety-category requirement.

This review identifies the EGCG hepatotoxicity threshold range of 140 mg/day to ~1,000 mg/day from published case reports, with substantial individual variability likely partly genetic.   - **DILIN data:** Navarro et al. 2014 (PMID 25043597 [funder: public]) — green tea extract repeatedly named among top single-ingredient supplement causes of liver injury in DILIN.
  - **Practical advice:** added warning signs (jaundice, dark urine, RUQ pain) and caution against pre-existing liver disease.
- **Sources:** Expanded from 3 → 8 to meet safety-category minimum. Mix of peer-reviewed reviews, EFSA/USP/Health Canada/FDA regulatory documents.
