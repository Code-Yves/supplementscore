### 123 — Why Most Vitamin D Studies Are Misleading  *(Reality Check)*
- **Status:** Material correction + clarity rewrite.
- **Fact-check error caught:** Original closing paragraph claimed "The D-HEALTH trial (60,000 IU monthly, 5 years) showed significant cardiovascular benefits." This is **incorrect**. D-HEALTH (Neale et al. 2022, *Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology*) found **no significant reduction** in major cardiovascular events. The cited Scragg 2017 paper is actually the VIDA trial — also null on CVD. Removed the false claim and replaced with verified positive findings: D2d (Pittas 2019, PMID 31173679 [funder: public]) showed a 62% reduction in progression to T2D in deficient adults; VITAL extension (Bischoff-Ferrari 2022, PMID 35139361 [funder: none_disclosed]) showed a 22% drop in autoimmune disease incidence.
- **Other claims:** "67% of VITAL participants had baseline 25(OH)D above 30 ng/mL" softened to "average baseline 25(OH)D was already 30.8 ng/mL" — more defensible against the published baseline characteristics.
- **Sources:** Expanded from 3 → 5, all with PMIDs.
