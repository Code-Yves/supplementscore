Articles page — hero treatment options

Four directions for an auto-rotating featured-article hero that sits between the page intro and the article list. Each rotates through ~15 hand-picked editor's-choice articles. All use real SupplementScore content; brand palette, light mode only.

A

Editorial Hero — single rotating card with proof element

Big, conventional, magazine-style. Headline + deck on the left, an interactive "proof" widget on the right (chart, stat panel, evidence summary — pulled from the article itself). Auto-rotates every ~7s with arrows + dots. The proof widget is what differentiates this from a generic carousel: every featured article has a "what does the data actually show" panel.

Picking between them

Best when… Tradeoff Build effort
A Articles have strong data tables / charts. The proof widget makes each rotation feel substantive, not promotional. Right-side widget needs a per-article custom snippet — adds editorial work. Medium-high
B You want users to see five articles immediately, biased toward the scanner / browser, not the deep reader. Less prestige than A or D — feels more "publication" than "journal." Low-medium
C Reinforces the "we’re reviewing studies in real time" positioning. Ticker is rare in this category, signals active editorial. Dark ticker breaks the cream/light theme; needs accessibility care for the marquee. Medium
D Maximum credibility / editorial gravitas. The "issue / vol. / no." framing positions SupplementScore as a publication, not a database. Implies a publishing cadence (issues) that may not match daily updates. Headline-heavy = less excerpt visible. Medium

My read: A or C is the closest fit to what the rest of the site already does — both lean on the "show me the data" instinct that makes SupplementScore distinct. B is the safest if you want maximum article exposure per scroll. D is the strongest brand bet if you want this page to feel like a flagship publication.
