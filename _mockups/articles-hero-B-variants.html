Refined: single hero + arrow nav

Option B without the 4 thumbnails. The dark teal editor’s-pick hero stays as the only feature; arrows let users page through all 15 articles. Four placement variants below — pick the one that feels right.

1

Arrows on the outside (railroad style)

Arrows sit OUTSIDE the hero, left and right, on cream/white pill buttons. Most familiar carousel pattern; clear hit area; never competes with the headline. Works well on desktop, hides on mobile (replaced by swipe + dots).

2

Arrows inside the corner (compact, in-card)

Arrows sit INSIDE the hero, top-right corner, on translucent buttons that read against the dark teal. A small "1 / 15" counter sits to their left. Quietest visual — keeps the hero feeling like one solid card. Best when you don’t want navigation chrome to compete with the headline.

3

Arrows + dots together, below the hero

All navigation lives in one row UNDER the hero: previous arrow, dot strip, next arrow, counter. Most accessible — controls are obvious, position is unambiguous, and the hero itself stays clean. Strongest if you want users to feel "this rotates and there’s more here."
