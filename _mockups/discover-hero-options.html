How they compare

Best when… Tradeoff Consistency w/ Articles hero
A You want Discover and Articles to feel like the same hero family. Same chrome, same controls, just different content type. Slight risk of feeling repetitive across the two tabs. Identical
B Discover should read more like an editorial column than a feed. Magazine quote treatment elevates the credibility of the “wild facts” positioning. Less visually punchy. Quieter on a busy page. Cousin, not twin
C You want the hero to lead with a number every time — turns each rotation into a mini stat-card. Most shareable visually. Requires every fact to have a punchy extracted number. A handful of qualitative facts ("FDA withdrew authorization") need rephrasing or get skipped. Same family, different shape

My read: A for fastest ship + perfect consistency with Articles. B if you want Discover to feel different in tone — more "essayist", less "feed". C if you’re willing to curate the fact pool down to 12-18 truly punchy stats and want the page to read as a stat showcase.
