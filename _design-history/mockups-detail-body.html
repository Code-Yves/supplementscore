What it is & what the evidence says

One of the most researched supplements in existence. Over 500 studies confirm it increases strength, power output, and lean muscle mass by saturating phosphocreatine stores in muscle.

A 2024 meta-analysis also confirmed it improves working memory in healthy adults. Safe for long-term use across all age groups with no serious side effects reported in decades of research.

How it works

Supplemental creatine increases the muscle's pool of phosphocreatine — the rapid-recharge fuel for ATP during short, high-intensity efforts. More phosphocreatine means a few extra reps before fatigue, which compounds into measurable strength and hypertrophy gains over weeks.

Dose

3–5 g/day continuously. Optional loading: 20 g/day for 5–7 days, then 3–5 g/day. Monohydrate is as effective as more expensive forms.

How to take

With water, any time of day. No food timing needed. Stay well hydrated.

Cycling & long-term use

Safe indefinitely. No cycling needed — one of the most studied for long-term safety.

Side effects & safety

Generally well-tolerated. ~5% of users experience mild GI discomfort during the loading phase. Minor water-weight gain (1–2 lb) in the first 2 weeks is normal. No documented effect on kidney function in healthy adults.
