Discover hero — three subtle treatments

Same content. Same fonts. Same colors. Each option adds one piece of editorial structure — masthead, asymmetric layout, or framing rules — that takes the hero from "plain" to "considered" without going back into AI-slop territory.

What it does: the masthead stripe gives the hero an editorial frame before any content reads. "Issue 04 · April 2026" plus the live "1,247 studies in the past 72h" stat does the credibility work the announcement bar used to do — without the pulsing dot. The stat moves out of the hero body and onto the masthead where it belongs.
Costs: ~40px of vertical space. Implies you commit to numbered "issues" — a small editorial discipline.

What it does: the hero stops being centered. Headline takes ~58% of the width on the left; lede sits in a narrower right column at the same baseline. The shape alone tells you this is editorial, not marketing — magazines do this; landing pages don't. The eyebrow gets a short rule prefix ("— THE FUN SIDE OF THE DATABASE") to anchor the left edge. Optional meta line under the lede ("Updated Apr 22 · 15 sections · 9 min read") replaces the deleted stat strip — but smaller and contextual.
Costs: on narrow screens it stacks back to single-column (no real loss). The headline reads as one paragraph, not three lines, so "733 supplements. 27,000+ studies." flows continuously.

What it does: a single solid 1px ink rule across the top defines where the hero begins. A hairline 0.5px below + a small byline ("Curated by the editorial team · Updated weekly · Apr 22, 2026") closes it. The hero reads as a single, frame-defined object on the page — like the masthead block of a printed feature.
Costs: almost none — adds ~30px of vertical space and one solid rule. The byline does small work that the announcement bar used to do loudly.

My take: Option 1 is the most editorial — it tells you Discover is a publication, which sets a tone the rest of the page already lives up to. Option 2 is the most distinctive — it costs the least and adds the most personality, but commits you to an asymmetric layout that may feel different from the rest of the site. Option 3 is the safest — quietest framing move with the smallest commitment, lands close to the current state with measurable polish.
