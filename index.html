Methodology & Transparency
How we score supplements, our limitations, and what we're working on
🔍
How we score supplements
Active
Every supplement is rated across 6 dimensions, each scored 1-5:
  • Efficacy — strength of clinical evidence for the primary claimed benefit
  • Safety — side effect profile, toxicity risk, and tolerability
  • Research depth — volume and quality of human clinical trials
  • Onset time — how quickly benefits are typically observed
  • Cost-effectiveness — value relative to evidence strength
  • Drug interaction risk — likelihood of interactions with medications
The composite score (out of 100) weights efficacy highest, followed by safety, then the remaining four equally. Only human clinical trial data is used — animal and in vitro studies inform context but never drive scores.
📚
Sources & search methodology
Active
We systematically search 14 peer-reviewed sources including Cochrane Library, PubMed/MEDLINE, NIH ODS, WHO, EFSA, and Examine.com. Our process:
  • Priority given to systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and Cochrane reviews
  • Individual RCTs used when no meta-analysis exists
  • Industry-funded studies flagged and weighted lower
  • Minimum inclusion: at least one human clinical trial for Tier 1-2 ratings
  • All data from 2024-2026 publication window with landmark older trials included
⚠️
Limitations & disclaimers
Active
  • This is an educational tool, not a medical device — it has not been reviewed or approved by the FDA, Health Canada, or any regulatory body
  • Scores reflect population-level evidence — individual responses vary based on genetics, diet, health status, and medications
  • AI-assisted review may miss nuances a trained clinician would catch
  • Supplement quality varies dramatically between brands — we do not test or endorse specific products
  • Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before starting, stopping, or changing any supplement
🧬
Clinical advisory board
Planned
We are actively recruiting licensed healthcare professionals (MDs, PharmDs, RDs) to review and validate our supplement ratings. A formal advisory board will provide:
  • Independent clinical review of all Tier 1 and Tier 2 ratings
  • Drug interaction verification by a licensed pharmacist
  • Pregnancy and pediatric safety review
  • Quarterly re-evaluation of evidence and scores
Interested professionals can reach out via the contact information below.
🔗
DOI/PMID citation links
In progress
We are adding direct links to the specific studies referenced in each supplement description. When complete, every claim will link to:
  • The PubMed entry (PMID) or DOI for the cited study
  • The Cochrane review or meta-analysis where applicable
  • The NIH, WHO, or EFSA guideline document referenced
This will allow anyone to verify our claims directly against the primary literature.

DNA-based personalisation
Planned
We are developing the ability to incorporate genetic data (from services like 23andMe or AncestryDNA) to refine recommendations based on:
  • MTHFR variants affecting folate metabolism
  • VDR polymorphisms affecting vitamin D requirements
  • CYP enzyme variations affecting drug/supplement metabolism
  • APOE status affecting cardiovascular supplement relevance
This feature will require additional clinical validation before release.
